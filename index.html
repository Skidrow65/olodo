Problems being faced in the community.

Kenally Ronson

Founder

Here are some of the problems that are being encountered within the community.
(a)Endless Treks: Imagine walking for miles under the scorching sun, carrying heavy containers, just to fetch water. Many people within this region face this reality daily.
(b)Time-Consuming: These long journeys steal precious time that could be spent on education, work, or leisure.
(c)Inadequate Water Supply: Many people within this region face this reality daily.
(d)Queues and Frustration: When multiple households rely on a single water source, queues form. People wait patiently, but frustration simmers as they jostle for their turn.
(e)Conflict: Tempers flare, especially during peak hours. Arguments erupt over who arrived first or whose turn it is.
(f)Drinking Water: The most fundamental need—clean drinking water—becomes scarce. Families ration it carefully, fearing it might run out.
(g)Hygiene and Cooking: Lack of water affects personal hygiene and cooking. Bathing, washing dishes, and preparing meals become challenging.
(h)Thirsty Livestock: Animals suffer alongside humans. Cattle, goats, and poultry need water to survive. Without it, they weaken, affecting livelihoods.
(i)Waterborne Diseases: Contaminated water breeds diseases like diarrhea, cholera, and dysentery. Children and the elderly are particularly vulnerable.
(j)Opportunity Cost: Time spent collecting water could be used for skill development or entrepreneurship.
(k)School Attendance: Children, especially girls, skip school to fetch water. Education takes a backseat to survival.
